Led Zeppelin Tribute Page

Bio

Led Zeppelin was an English rock band formed in London in 1968, comprised of guitarist Jimmy Page, singer Robert Plant, Bassist and keyboardist John Paul Jones, and drummer John Bonham.

The band's heavy, guitar-driven sound has led them to be cited as one of the main predecessors of heavy metal. The band themselves drew from a wide range of influences including blues, psychedelia, and folk music.

Led Zeppelin has been widely considered one of the most successful and influential rock groups in history, and are also one of the best selling music artists of all time.

In 1995, they were inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame. The museum's description of the band states they were as influential during the 1970s as the Beatles were during the 1960s.
